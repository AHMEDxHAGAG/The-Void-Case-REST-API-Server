# THE STARRY-EYED CASE
**Setting:** A theatre car — on set, backstage, and the lighting bridge
**Victim:** The Lead Actor

## Overview

During what appears to be a live performance or rehearsal, The Lead Actor is shot through the chest with a single bullet at close range (1–3 metres). The bullet passes through his body and lodges in the set wall behind a replica of Van Gogh's *The Starry Night*. A note found near the body reads *"You'll pay for betraying me"* — it is not a prop from the show, and the bullet passed through it.

A third character is found unconscious backstage, holding the revolver identified as the murder weapon. The apparent framing is clear: the unconscious person is meant to look guilty. The actual sequence of events is more complicated.

## The Setup

The show being performed involves a scene where The Gun Actor shoots The Lead Actor on stage. The Lead Actor was supposed to operate a remote switch at the exact moment of the scene, detonating a satchel of fake blood hidden inside his costume to simulate being shot. The switch was found discarded in a rubbish bin backstage.

The gun used in the show is a replica that fires only blanks — found in a two-gun case backstage (left side, near the set door), swimming in oil to work its mechanism. The case fits two guns. Only this replica was in it.

The real revolver — an old model with five unused bullets remaining — was found in the hands of The Unconscious Third Character backstage right, who had been struck multiple times in the back of the head.

## Key Points of Investigation

1. **The fake blood switch was discarded.** The Lead Actor never triggered his own blood satchel during the scene. Either he was already dead when he should have triggered it, or the switch was taken and discarded by someone who wanted to prevent it from going off — because real blood was going to be spilled instead.

2. **The second gun is missing from the case.** The replica gun case holds two. Only the blank-firing replica remains. The real revolver was the second gun — and it came from somewhere. Whether it was always in the theatre or brought in is unclear.

3. **The oil trail backstage right.** Oil is smeared along the back wall as if someone's hand ran along it. The replica gun is also submerged in oil. The oil connects backstage left (where the replica and its oil are) to backstage right (where the unconscious body and the real revolver are). Someone moved between these areas.

4. **The Understudy was framed.** Struck multiple times in the back of the head, then had the revolver placed in their hands. They did not shoot anyone — they were set up to appear guilty.

5. **The note is personal, not a prop.** *"You'll pay for betraying me."* This was brought by the killer specifically. It was placed at The Lead Actor's position and shot through when the bullet was fired at close range. The killer knew exactly where The Lead Actor would be standing.

6. **Shot from 1–3 metres.** This is an intimate distance. The killer was on or immediately adjacent to the stage during the performance.

## Notes

The oil trail, the missing second gun slot, the discarded remote switch, and the personal note are the four physical pillars of the case. The framing of The Understudy is apparent from the head wounds; the killer is someone else who was on or near the stage during the scene.
