# THE H.O.P.E. CASE
**Setting:** A facility with a prison, chemistry lab, library, and train room
**Victim:** The Chef
**The Blackened:** Unknown — the case file does not name the killer

---

## Overview

The Chef is found burned alive inside a prison cell. An empty bucket suspended above her body by rope — connected by a very long rope to the train room — was used to deliver an incendiary chemical solution onto her. The rope, when pulled by the movement of the train, tipped the bucket and ignited the contents. The killer engineered a remote, time-delayed execution mechanism using chemistry: a potassium chlorate solution synthesized from common materials and triggered without ever needing to be present in the cell.

The vents in The Chef's room had been boarded shut beforehand — preventing any escape.

---

## The Mechanism

The killer synthesized potassium chlorate (KClO₃) — an oxidizing agent that ignites combustible materials — using equipment from the chemistry lab. The synthesis procedure was found written in a note in the lab:

```
Ca(ClO)₂ tablets + water + heat
Filter the solution
Heat the solution + KCl + water
Filter the solution
Wash insoluble materials with water
Room temperature
Cool the beaker
Wash crystals with cold water
→ KClO₃
```

**Materials available in the chemistry lab:**
- Calcium hypochlorite tablets (empty bucket found — used as bleaching agent / pool cleaner)
- Salt-free salt (potassium chloride, KCl — a common sodium chloride substitute)
- Hot plates (for heating reactions)
- Filter setups (gravity filtration with flask and filter paper)
- Blow torch

The synthesized potassium chlorate was placed in a bucket, which was suspended by rope above The Chef's position inside the cell. A second, much longer rope was glued to the bottom of the bucket and run all the way to the train room — 58 metres total, composed of several ropes tied together. The rope was tied to a door handle on the train. When the train moved, the door handle turned, pulling the long rope and tipping the bucket — dousing The Chef in the chemical and igniting it.

The train only moves when someone is inside it.

## Key Points of Investigation

1. **The rope runs 58 metres to the train room.** The train only moves when occupied — someone started the train. That person triggered the mechanism, whether they knew it or not, or they triggered it intentionally.

2. **The synthesis note in the chemistry lab** was left behind — either carelessly or deliberately. It contains the exact procedure used. Anyone with chemistry knowledge, or access to the library's chemistry books, could have followed it.

3. **The empty calcium hypochlorite bucket and depleted KCl** confirm the synthesis took place. The filter setups were used. The blow torch was available.

4. **The boarded vents** were prepared in advance. This is not improvised — The Chef was meant to have no escape.

5. **The knockout gas** in The Chef's room suggests she may have been incapacitated before being placed or locked in the cell, removing any chance of resistance during setup.

6. **The distance and geometry:** 23 metres between the train's starting position and the bucket. The train travels a circular path with radius 17 metres. The circumference of that path is approximately 107 metres. At what point in the train's movement does the rope become taut enough to tip the bucket? This is a solvable calculation — the exact moment of death can be reverse-engineered from physics.
